Insights

Insights drive human-centered design. Supply side insights were gleaned from first-hand conversations with /players who shape the back-end of our food chain. Consumer-facing insights surfaced from conversations with over 120 Target customers who interacted with our TEN scale and Good and Gather brand.

Supply Side

Insight 1: Transparency is Valuable to Changemakers.
Insight 2: Seasonality Matters
Insight 3: We're All in this Together

Changemakers value transparency

Across the board, industry influencers large and small are taking a stance against the status-quo. Brands that are selling a message of honesty have created their own truth-telling devices & processes to ensure value for their customers.

''People should get rewarded for doing the right thing… you can’t tell half the story and get rewarded all the same''

- Mary, Follow the Honey

''Customers will pay more once they hear the story of how the honey was made?''

- Mary, Follow the Honey

Seasonality
Matters

Nature’s supply doesn’t always fit demand. Today’s food producers suffer from menu-driven markets, and consumers who expect consistency. This mismatch in demand discourages diversity, stifles local production, and creates food waste at a large scale.

''The industry is menu driven… I wish it was ocean driven. It’s squid season and people should know that.''

- Dave, fisherman

''Food produce is way more complex than people understand because of seasonality, supply / demand, and arrangement in grocery stores.''

- Mike, Target Produce Buyer

We’re all in this
together

Working towards a better food system is a group efforts. A better food system is built Changing our food system is a gargantuan task - one that no single producer, distributor, or retailer can take on alone.

''I’m sitting here representing all the local fisherman.''

-Dave, fisherman

[when speaking about suppliers]
“I believe in marriages, not one night stands.''

- Mary, Follow the Honey

Consumer Side

Insight 1: Price Motivates.
Insight 2: Information Overload
Insight 3: Food Reflects lifestyle

Price Motivates

Health, freshness, and price are the 3 prevailing factors that influence choice when it comes to food - with price at the forefront. Food is a commodity in today’s world, and the price tag can distort or hide the real story.

''I shop here and at Stop and Shop - wherever has better deals.''

- Target Customers

''It’s all about price''

- Target Customers

Information Overload

Consumers are overwhelmed by the mass of messaging when it comes to their food. What does local mean? Is organic better? Customers feel helpless navigating the landscape of food, but are reluctant to engage with more data - unless it helps them make a decision.

''I don’t even know what healthy means... I just want to know if this avocado is good for me''

- Target Customers

''Organic just means healthy right?''

- Target Customers

Food Reflects lifestyle

The foods we choose speak to who we think we are - and who we aspire to be. Decisions about what restaurants we dine at and the snacks we grab on the go are as much about health as they are about our values and perceived self-image.

''The people who eat organic are the same people who jog. It’s about well-being… How do you make people feel better about the lifestyle that comes with healthy food?''

-Caitrin, Provender
